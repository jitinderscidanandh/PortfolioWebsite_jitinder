Chapter Zero

The story so far

It started on a cricket pitch. Years of competitive cricket taught me the things no MBA does — preparation, reading the game, and staying calm when everything's on the line. Then I traded the pitch for products, and it turns out the game is the same: you play the long innings.

Ten years ago I started on the front lines of banking — managing corporate-salary clients at Kotak, then running Video KYC for credit cards at BankBazaar. Unglamorous, regulated, detail-heavy work. It's also exactly why I can build fintech products that survive contact with the RBI.

In 2021 I joined NOVAC (Shriram Group's tech arm) as an Associate Product Owner with one web journey to my name. Today I lead the entire Digital B2C & B2B2C portfolio for India's largest NBFC — a 120-person ecosystem building the Shriram One Super App, Super Web, mZIVA and Fortune Partner One. Three promotions, a dozen 0→1 launches, and a few billion rupees of digital business later, I still get a kick out of a clean funnel chart.

The year's scoreboard: UPI cracked the national top 40, BBPS the top 20, and the personal-loan program I direct added ₹1,300+ crore in yearly disbursals. And because scaling wasn't enough, we started two businesses from zero on the side — a merchant-acquiring line (mZIVA) and a partner super-app for the whole Shriram Group.

Chapter Four
The merger & the web years
Senior Product Owner · NOVAC, on Shriram Finance
Oct 2023 – Jan 2025

Two companies became one, and someone had to merge their digital front doors. I led the fusion of STFC and SCUF's platforms into the single Shriram Finance website — 19 lakh visits at launch — then rebuilt the fixed-deposit journey so thoroughly (FD 5.0: one-step flow, OTP-based KYC, cheque OCR) that online FD issuance touched ₹2,809 crore, 40% of it from people who'd never banked with Shriram before.

₹1,331 Cr → ₹2,574 Cr → ₹2,809 Cr
digital FD issuance across my three years on the journey
  • Owned multiple digital assets across the web ecosystem — corporate website, Digital FD & RD journeys, and Customer & Service Portals.
  • Spearheaded the high-visibility merger of STFC & SCUF digital platforms into the unified Shriram Finance website — 19 lakh visits, 1.2 lakh loan enquiries and 16,000 new depositors at launch.
  • Led the FD 5.0 revamp — "One Stepper Flow", OTP-based CKYC, PAN masking, Cheque-OCR name matching — lifting online FD issuance to ₹2,809 Cr (40% new-to-bank) and integrating BBPS into the website.
  • Launched Fixed Deposit 3.0 and Shriram One Gold & Silver; built an omni-channel App ↔ SuperWeb experience — 30% higher engagement, 25% better release efficiency, 40% rise in customer satisfaction.
  • Led and mentored a 3-member product team; owned UAT sign-off and release-readiness presentations to senior management.

The Craft

What I'm actually good at

Zero to one

My favourite phase: nothing exists yet. mZIVA, Fortune Partner One, the merchant business — I've taken products from whiteboard to revenue repeatedly, including the unglamorous parts: settlement rails, vendor contracts, ops portals.

One to a hundred

Scaling is a different sport — North Star metrics, OKR cascades, funnel surgery, A/B discipline. It's how UPI grew 208% and a loan program found ₹1,300 crore of extra disbursals in a single year.

Regulated by design

RBI, NPCI, IRDAI, SEBI — I build with the regulator in the room, not as an afterthought. Ten years in BFSI means compliance is muscle memory, from KYC flows to TPAP license mandates.

Leading the many

120+ people, 12 direct reports, four squads, a Scrum-of-Scrums cadence, and a monthly steering committee with business, tech and risk leadership. I keep large systems of people moving in one direction.

Decisions from data

Cohort tables, risk-bucket restructuring, propensity models, opportunity sizing — the ₹1,500 Cr growth plan I presented to the JMD was built row by row, not vibe by vibe.

Growing people

Three of my associates have become owners of their own products. Backlog grooming, sprint rituals and honest retros — the boring rituals, done well, are how teams get good.
